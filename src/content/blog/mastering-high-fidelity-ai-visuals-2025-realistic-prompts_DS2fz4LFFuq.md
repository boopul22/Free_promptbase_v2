## How to Structure a High-Fidelity Prompt

Think of a prompt like a recipe. If you forget an ingredient, the dish fails. To get **lifelike AI image generation**, follow this four-part structure:

*   **The Core Subject:** Who or what is the focus? Be specific about the person's identity and action.
*   **Technical Camera Settings:** Use terms like "35mm lens" or "f/1.8" to tell the AI how much blur you want in the background.
*   **Environment & Lighting:** Instead of "bright," say "golden hour sunlight filtering through sheer curtains."
*   **Stylistic Modifiers:** Add words like "lifestyle editorial" or "raw film grain" for that final touch of realism.

## Tips for Refining and Iterating Your Results

Rarely is the first image perfect. Use these three "pro" moves:
*   **Negative Prompting:** Explicitly tell the AI what *not* to include, like "oversaturated colors" or "cartoonish features."
*   **Aspect Ratios:** Always define your ratio (9:16 for Reels, 4:5 for Instagram posts) to ensure the composition is correct.
*   **The "Seed" Method:** If you find a face or style you love, use that image's "seed number" to keep your characters consistent across different scenes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What makes a prompt "realistic" versus "artistic"?
A realistic prompt focuses on technical photography elements (lens type, lighting physics, skin texture), whereas an artistic prompt uses descriptive, emotive language (dreamy, ethereal, vibrant) that might ignore the laws of physics.

### 2. Do these prompts work on both mobile and desktop versions of Gemini?
Yes. Gemini’s core engine is the same across platforms, though the desktop version sometimes offers more advanced settings for image generation.

### 3. Are Meta AI images copyright-free for commercial use?
Currently, AI-generated images have complex copyright laws that vary by country. Generally, most platforms allow personal use, but for commercial use, you should check the latest Terms of Service as they update frequently in 2025.

### 4. How do I fix "AI hallucinations" in human faces?
Try to be more specific about the "gaze" and "expression." If the eyes look wrong, add "focused eyes, natural eye reflection" to your prompt. Small details often correct the AI's logic.

### 5. Why are my prompt results looking blurry or filtered?
This often happens if the AI thinks you want a "cinematic" look but overdoes the bokeh. Try adding "sharp focus, high resolution, 8k, detailed textures" to sharpen the image.

### 6. What is the "Nanobanana" prompting style?
The **Nanobanana** style (popularized by @rahulx3) focuses on "lifestyle realism." It prioritizes images that look like candid, high-end iPhone or Leica photography rather than "over-designed" AI art.

### 7. How often does Meta AI update its rendering engine?
Meta typically pushes "silent" updates to its Llama-based image models every few months to improve anatomical accuracy and prompt adherence.

### 8. Can I use these prompts for short-form video generation?
Many of these descriptive prompts work excellently as "starting frames" for AI video tools like Sora or Runway Gen-3, providing a high-quality base for animation.
