## How to Use Nano Banana Prompts Across Different AI Platforms

While the prompts are powerful, knowing where to paste them is key to success.

### Implementing Prompts in Google Gemini
Google Gemini (especially the Advanced and Nano versions) excels at understanding structural context. When using the prompts above, it’s best to give Gemini a "persona" first. Tell it: *"You are a professional photographer and social media strategist."* Then, paste your "Banana" prompt. It will give you options for variations you haven't even thought of.

### Optimizing Outputs for Meta AI (Instagram/WhatsApp)
Meta AI is built for speed and integration. If you’re inside Instagram, you can use these prompts to generate images directly for your Stories. Since Meta AI prefers shorter interactions, you might need to break a long Nano Banana prompt into two parts: first the visual description, then the lighting details.

### Customizing Prompts for Personal Branding
Don't just copy and paste! To maintain your personal brand, swap out the character descriptions for your own features. If the prompt asks for a "man in a fur coat," change it to "a woman in a denim jacket" to fit your style while keeping the "Cinematic 35mm" lighting instructions.

Sometimes AI gets weird—it might give your character six fingers or a strange background. To fix this, use "Negative Prompts" in your follow-up message. Simply type: *"Great, but remove the digital smoothing and fix the hands."*
